# The Marriage Crisis in Egypt: Gold Prices & Economic Challenges 💍📈

Hey everyone! Welcome to our repository. This is a project we did for the Data Analysis Course at the Egypt University of Informatics (EUI). 

We wanted to look into something super relevant right now: how crazy gold prices and the economy are affecting young Egyptians who want to get married. 

## Project Overview 📊
Basically, getting married in Egypt is getting really expensive. We wanted to see how the traditional *Shabka* (gold gift) and the housing crisis are changing things for young people (aged 18–28). 

We surveyed 128 people, mostly university students and fresh grads, to see what they think about marriage costs today and if they are open to changing traditions.

## Research Questions & Hypotheses 🤔
**Main Question:** How are rising gold prices and the high cost of living affecting the ability of young Egyptians to get married?

**Our Hypothesis:** We thought that most young people would find the current costs totally unrealistic. We also predicted that a lot of them would be open to making traditions simpler (like changing or getting rid of the Shabka) to make things easier.

## Repository Structure 📂
Here's what you'll find in this repo:
* `clean_data.csv`: The cleaned dataset we used for our analysis (128 anonymous survey responses).
* `The Marriage Crisis.ipynb`: Our Jupyter Notebook showing all the technical stuff—how we cleaned the data, renamed columns, and made our charts.
* `Research_Report.pdf`: The full academic report with our methodology and detailed findings.

## What We Found (Key Findings) 💡
1. **The Realism Gap:** A huge 61.7% of the people we asked think current marriage costs are "completely unrealistic" for fresh graduates. Only 7.8% think it's actually manageable.
2. **The Biggest Barriers:** Everyone talks about gold, but the data showed something else is the biggest problem:
   * **Apartment Costs:** 49.2% (The #1 barrier)
   * **Gold (Shabka):** 24.2%
   * **Furniture/Setup:** 19.5%
3. **Changing Traditions:** 
   * **Openness to Change:** Over 60% of people said they are open to replacing the gold Shabka with silver, or even canceling it completely.
   * **Modest Expectations:** 43% think a reasonable Shabka nowadays is just 1 to 20 grams of gold.
4. **Impact on Society:** 
   * **Delayed Marriage:** 93% agreed that all this economic pressure is making people marry much later.
   * **Emigration:** A lot of people chose "Work or move abroad" as their main plan to afford marriage, which shows how tough the local situation is right now.

## Methodology 🛠️
* **Who we asked:** Young Egyptians (18–28), mostly urban university students.
* **How we did it:** Convenience sampling using social media and university groups.
* **Data Cleaning:** We had to remove some "troll" answers and standardize the text responses.
* **Analysis:** We mostly used descriptive stats (like finding the Mode) to spot trends in the categorical data.
